Market Potential Analysis

In this project, I conducted a Whitespace Analysis by comparing a client‚Äôs existing grocery store POIs with a broader POI universe. The goal was to identify untapped opportunities by performing data matching based on location and attributes, then classifying stores into Potential Channel, Existing & Matched, and Existing & Unmatched. This provided clear insights into expansion opportunities as well as areas requiring data quality validation.

Next, I carried out a Grid Analysis by dividing the target area into spatial grids using H3 geohash level 8. Each grid was evaluated based on the number of POIs, consumer expenditure, and people traffic, which were then converted into scores. The scoring framework categorized grids into five levels of attractiveness, effectively distinguishing between Acceptable and Unacceptable areas for strategic placement.

Finally, I performed a Market Potential Analysis to estimate store-level opportunities within a 250-meter walking catchment area. Telco mobility data was used to measure active devices as a proxy for population or households, which was then multiplied by average food expenditure. This approach generated a market size estimation for each store and classified them as Potential or Not Potential, providing a quantitative basis for expansion and investment decisions.

POI Index Growth Analysis

I measured POI density at two time points (June 2024 and December 2025) by aggregating all POIs into H3 hexagonal grids at resolution 8 (~500 m). For each hexagon, POI counts were calculated and normalized into a POI Index to allow fair comparison across areas. Growth was measured as both absolute and percentage change between the two periods. The results were visualized through heatmaps/choropleth maps and change distribution charts.

The analysis highlighted high-growth hex clusters (micro-level expansion opportunities) as well as declining hexes (potential areas of reduced activity). Deliverables included 500 m‚Äìlevel change maps, top/bottom hex tables with percentage shifts, and recommendations for priority zones in market expansion or field validation. The index framework is scalable and can be reused for continuous monitoring.

House Price Prediction Using Deep Learning

This project aimed to predict property prices in Surabaya by combining structured transaction data with unstructured satellite images. The dataset included 1,842 records remained. Satellite images were cropped around each property location, resized to 224√ó224 pixels, and normalized for modeling. A hybrid approach was implemented by extracting high-level visual features from satellite images using VGG16 and merging them with structured features scaled via MinMaxScaler. Data was split into 80% training and 20% validation. The model demonstrated that combining tabular attributes with spatial image features improved predictive accuracy, capturing hidden value factors such as vegetation, building textures, and road accessibility.
